Thermal conductivity is measured in watts per kelvin-meter. -<xsd:annotation>
 <xsd:documentation>Dimensional response to temperature change is expressed by its coefficient of thermal expansion. When the temperature of a substance changes, the energy that is stored in the intermolecular bonds between atoms changes. When the stored energy increases, so does the length of the molecular bonds. As a result, solids typically expand in response to heating and contract on cooling. The degree of expansion divided by the change in temperature is called the material's coefficient of thermal expansion and generally varies with temperature.</xsd:documentation>
